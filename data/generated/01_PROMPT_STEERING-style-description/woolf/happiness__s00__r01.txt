Was it balancing one thing against another, she asked? Was it a quiet weighing, a subtle tug, a slow, silent pull that kept him steady, that kept her unsteady, that kept the world in a fragile, trembling balance, that kept the thread, that kept the petal, that kept the whole? He answered, not with a word, but with a motion, a tilt of head, a slight lift of lips, a shift of weight from one foot to the other, a small, almost imperceptible, flick of the wrist, a gesture that said, “I feel it,” and she heard it, heard the echo, heard the faint, almost inaudible, resonance that ran through the room, ran through her, ran through the air, ran through the space between them, ran through the space that had been filled with silence, that had been filled with the soft, low hum of life, that had been filled with the steady, quiet rhythm of breath.
